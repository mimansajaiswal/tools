    Notes & Assumptions

    How to read the outputs

    • All values are cumulative through each column date.
    • This Year rows show the incremental value added in that column period.
    • Mode meaning: Direct mode compares Company A vs Company B with Delta = A minus B. Promotion mode compares A->A, B->B, A->B, B->A with deltas versus B->B.
    • Net Worth + Delta: shown as after-tax cumulative values at each milestone date.
    • Exclude living expenses from net worth + delta: toggles whether living expense drag is included in displayed net worth/delta values (breakdown component rows still show modeled components).
    • Living expense assumptions: Living Expenses /YR applies annually and Living Expenses ↑/YR steps annual living expenses higher each year; use Prorated for partial year to control day-level proration for partial periods.
    • Living expenses required timing: Choose At Start, Per Paycheck, or By Year End to control when the living-expense buffer is held before investing.
    • Salary Cash: after-tax base pay after 401k, Backdoor IRA, ESPP, and Mega Backdoor Roth payroll contributions.
    • Market Investment: invested market exposure (salary-premium investing and/or invested sign-on/bonus cash, depending on path/paradigm).
    • RSU: value of vested shares kept after sell-to-cover taxes.
    • ESPP: market value of purchased shares at exit; purchase cost is already deducted from Salary Cash. This can show $0 before the first purchase date even if payroll ESPP contributions have started.
    • Growth drivers: Scenario Stock Growth % drives equity-style assets (e.g., RSU/stock-linked investments). Scenario Market Growth % drives retirement bucket compounding.
    • 401k Employee: employee 401k deferrals compounded using Scenario Market Growth %.
    • 401k Match: employer match compounded using Scenario Market Growth %.
    • Backdoor IRA: post-tax contribution converted to Roth IRA and compounded using Scenario Market Growth %.
    • Mega Backdoor Roth: after-tax 401k contribution value under the 415(c) limit, compounded using Scenario Market Growth %.
    • Cash (Sign-on + Bonus): after-tax sign-on and bonus paid as cash. Invested Sign-on + Bonus: that same after-tax cash invested in Company B stock.
    • Taxes: cumulative modeled taxes on W-2 cashflows. Shown below totals for interpretation (totals are already after-tax), and partial periods are pro-rated by actual days/pay periods.
    • Non-taxable: shown in the Net Worth table and currently includes Roth balances (Backdoor IRA + Mega Backdoor Roth) plus HSA medical spending.
    • Allocation Transparency table: Target is modeled desired contribution, Funded is actual achieved contribution, and Unmet is remaining gap after all constraints.
    • Timeline Mix: bar colors correspond to timeline segments listed in the legend directly above the chart.
    • Sensitivity card: uses current scenario settings while sweeping growth/tenure inputs.
    • Growth pattern fallback: if Stock/Market Growth has fewer values than tenure, remaining years use the average of the entered rates.

    Assumptions: partner pre-tax 401k input reduces household federal taxable income; 401k match uses the IRS compensation limit ($360k in 2026, +$5k/year); retirement buckets use Scenario Market Growth %; Mega Backdoor room is 415(c) minus employee 401k and employer match, so it can decrease if match grows faster than your 415(c) increase setting; ESPP uses the configured offering windows and can appear as $0 before first purchase even when payroll deductions have started; HSA/FSA/healthcare premiums are modeled as pre-tax payroll deductions (with IRS limits), healthcare premiums are deducted before living-expense flooring, and HSA retained balance compounds at Scenario Market Growth %.
